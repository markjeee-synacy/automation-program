# Initiative Service Management

## Purpose

Build an automated service management process, from inquiry, to order taking, then activation, and ending at deactivation based on the WL platform.

Covers the following concerns:

1. Service life cycle
2. Site acquisition
3. Service feasibility
4. Service quotation and order taking

## Objectives

### 2026 — [AUTO-84](https://utinternational.jira.com/browse/AUTO-84)

- **How may we reliably report our historical performance for all services sold ever since we started.** ([AUTO-89](https://utinternational.jira.com/browse/AUTO-89))

  This is a follow-up objective after the roll-out of Service Records in WL. In addition to data migration, we will also be validating information for accuracy and completing gaps if any. After this, all reporting based on SR data set will only source from WL.

- **How may we properly record all OTC payables for services sold.** ([AUTO-90](https://utinternational.jira.com/browse/AUTO-90))

  This is an expansion of SR in WL feature, to properly record OTC information, such as its purpose, amount, as well as automate recording based on the state/changes of a service.

- **How may we efficiently check for feasibility of a service as part of a sales development process.** ([AUTO-102](https://utinternational.jira.com/browse/AUTO-102))

  Aim is to consolidate network, TPS records, and Fiber network information in WL, so it can automatically answer inquires for service feasibility. For edge cases, provide an integrated workflow for requesting manual feasibility check, and record results which can be referred to in later inquiries.

- **How may we accurately record customer requirements, product selection, and configuration, as part of an automated service order workflow.** ([AUTO-91](https://utinternational.jira.com/browse/AUTO-91))

  Aim is to standardized and automate the service quoting and order taking process, and implement the feature in WL.
