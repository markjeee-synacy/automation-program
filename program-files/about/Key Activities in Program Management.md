# Key Activities in Automation Program Management

This document outlines the key activities involved in managing the Automation Program at RISE. It serves as a reference for program managers, team members, and stakeholders to understand the core functions and responsibilities that drive the program's success.

---

## 1. Strategy & Roadmap Development

The program's north star function — defining where the program is going and how it gets there.

- Assess the current state of automation maturity across RISE's operations
- Define the target vision and build a phased roadmap aligned to the department's strategic priorities
- Publish the **Annual Roadmap** each year, listing planned objectives within initiatives scoped for that year
- Maintain the **Operational Roadmap** in the AUTO Jira board as the live, working view of all objectives in execution
- Maintain **Program Roadmaps** (EA, BI, NI) and **Initiative Roadmaps**, including unscheduled ideas and upcoming opportunities
- Sequence objectives through the Strategy process: **Intention → Develop → Active → Done → Archived**
- Run reality checks and revise the roadmap when conditions, delays, or strategic shifts occur

---

## 2. Opportunity Identification & Development

The upstream engine of the program — ensuring the right work gets into the pipeline.

- Proactively discover signals of inefficiency, manual effort, data gaps, and service quality issues from across the business
- Capture all incoming ideas into the pipeline without filtering at intake (**Idea** stage), preserving a broad possibility space
- Evaluate ideas for strategic alignment, commercial value, and technical feasibility (**Opportunity** stage)
- Develop qualified opportunities into a concrete problem scope, desired outcomes, and solution approach (**Analysis** stage)
- Produce a realization decision — go/no-go — before any planning or resources are committed
- Determine the work item type: **Story** (feature/build), **Study** (analytical recommendation), or **Task** (data/maintenance/ops)
- Group related work items under a **Workstream** for traceability within an objective

---

## 3. Initiative & Objective Scoping

Translating strategic intent into structured, executable commitments.

- Define **Initiatives** at the strategic level — vision-articulated, domain-scoped, multi-quarter, with a working group
- Break each initiative into **Objectives** — specific, measurable outcomes scoped to 1–2 quarters, with an assigned Objective Lead
- Maintain the **Initiative → Objective → Work Item** hierarchy in Confluence and Jira
- Ensure each objective is aligned, committed, and published to the Operational Roadmap before work begins (**Active** stage)
- Identify cross-initiative dependencies, conflicts, and resource constraints at scoping time — not after

---

## 4. Cross-Functional Execution & Delivery

Driving the Develop process from Planning through to validated Impact.

- Plan resources, dependencies, and delivery timelines for each committed work item (**Planning** stage)
- Execute through iterative design, build, testing, and staged rollout (**Implementation** stage)
- Monitor all active Jira boards — EA, BI, NI, AUTO — for objective and work item status, velocity, and blockers
- Escalate risks proactively — minimum one sprint before impact, always with a recommended resolution path
- Assess delivered outcomes against defined success criteria and close the impact delta (**Review** stage)
- Close and archive completed or discontinued items with documented outcomes (**Done → Archived**)

---

## 5. Governance, Standards & Reusability

Ensuring the program builds in a consistent, scalable, and maintainable way.

- Establish and evolve automation standards and tooling guidelines across EA, BI, and NI
- Define reusable frameworks, patterns, and templates that reduce rework across initiatives
- Govern the Develop and Org Dev process lifecycles — evolving them as the program matures
- Maintain the Program Manager's gate function: evaluate and approve ideas and opportunities for roadmap inclusion, balancing value, feasibility, and resource capacity

---

## 6. Sync Facilitation & Stakeholder Communication

Keeping execution aligned and stakeholders informed.

- Prepare and facilitate recurring syncs: **BSS** (bi-weekly Mon 4PM), **OSS** (alternating Mon 4PM), **BI** (bi-weekly Tue 4PM), **NI** (alternating Tue 4PM)
- Report objective status at the **Tech Leaders meeting**
- Capture meeting notes and actions filed in the correct Google Drive folders per domain
- Serve as the primary program voice to the Head of Automation, CTO, and stakeholders
- Produce concise executive briefings on program health, risks, and decisions — recommendation-led, no ambiguity
- Synthesize signals from BSS, OSS, BI, and NI into coherent program-level narratives

---

## 7. Performance Tracking & Continuous Improvement

Closing the loop between delivery and future planning.

- Monitor initiative and objective outcomes against defined KPIs — cost savings, efficiency gains, error reduction
- Benchmark delivery against roadmap commitments; surface variances and trends
- Produce health reports, retrospectives, and risk assessments
- Feed lessons learned and performance insights back into the roadmap — what to continue, adjust, or stop
- Use data to prioritize, deprioritize, or kill initiatives that are not delivering value

---

## 8. Organizational Development (Org Dev)

Supporting adoption of change when transformation extends beyond technical delivery.

- Apply structured change management when an initiative requires stakeholder alignment, role redesign, training, or communication programs
- Assess change impact and readiness before and during implementation
- Lead Org Dev activities within the Automation program on a case-by-case basis
- Evolve the Org Dev process and methods as the program's change management practice matures

---

## 9. Knowledge Management

Ensuring decisions, work, and progress are permanently traceable.

- Maintain Confluence as the canonical knowledge base — structured as: **Program → Roadmap → Initiative → Objective**
- Document all decisions and actions within 48 hours in Google Drive and Atlassian
- Keep meeting notes and transcriptions filed in the correct Drive folders per program domain
- Ensure the Initiative Roadmap includes unscheduled ideas, maintaining a live pipeline view
