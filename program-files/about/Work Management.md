# Work Management

Work Management in Automation is grouped based on the following:

## Roadmap

This is a high-level roadmap presentation of the different objectives and key results. An objective is either an impact a program is aiming to achieve or an operations performance goal, while key results are the specific outcomes achieved through project and operational work that provide a measure of whether the objective has been met. Roadmaps follow the Strategy process in Automation.

We maintain 4 kinds of roadmaps, as follows:

1. **Annual Roadmap** — Lists the planned objectives within initiatives, scoped to be achieved within a given year. A new version is published each year (e.g., 2025, 2026), serving as the baseline plan for what the year aims to deliver.
2. **Operational Roadmap** — The live, working view of objectives in execution, maintained in the Automation Roadmap Jira board. It consolidates objectives from the current and previous years, structured with initiatives as parent groupings and objectives as sub-tasks. All up-to-date status and progress tracking happens here.
3. **Program Roadmap** — Lists the different initiatives defined under a specific program (e.g., Enterprise Automation, Business Intelligence, Network Intelligence) to achieve its goals. This provides the program-level view of what initiatives exist and how they contribute to the program's direction.
4. **Initiative Roadmap** — Lists the different objectives within a single initiative, as well as upcoming ideas or opportunities that have not yet been scheduled for effort.

## Programs

Programs are created to fulfill a business need that the Technology department is responsible for, and exist for as long as that need persists.

Program management primarily follows the Develop process in Automation, where work is focused on evaluating ideas and opportunities, and scheduling resources for implementation. The Strategy process complements this by guiding the creation of Initiatives and Objectives, serving as the method for prioritizing impact and allocating resources.

### Initiatives

Initiatives are defined from the strategic perspective of a program and are intended to align resources towards a common long-term goal. They are used for large-scale scopes of work, typically spanning multiple quarters and potentially recurring across years as new objectives are defined under them.

An Initiative has the following characteristics:

1. A goal, articulated as a vision
2. A specific domain scope
3. A timeline, enumerated per 6-month period (H1, H2)
4. A working group of implementers and collaborators

### Objectives

Objectives are the defined outcomes within an Initiative. Each objective represents a specific, measurable result that the initiative is aiming to achieve, scoped to be completed within 1 to 2 quarters. An objective is assigned to an objective lead who is accountable for driving it to completion. The actual work to achieve an objective is carried out through work items managed in the respective program boards. Objectives are what get planned into the Annual Roadmap, tracked in the Operational Roadmap, and measured as key results — providing the evidence that an initiative is progressing towards its goal.

Objectives are assigned an Objective Lead.

## Work Item

Work items are the individual units of work that contribute to achieving an objective. They are managed in the respective program boards in Jira (e.g., EA, BI, NI) and are linked to their parent objective in the Operational Roadmap through the Jira link field. Each work item progresses through a defined set of stages in the Develop process, from initial identification through to completion.

A work item can represent different types of effort — such as a development task, an analysis activity, a system integration, or a process improvement — depending on the nature of the objective it supports. Multiple work items may be linked to a single objective, collectively forming the body of work needed to achieve that defined outcome. Progress and updates on work items are tracked through comments and status changes in the program board, which in turn inform the overall status of the objective.

Work Item are assigned a Development Lead.
